Non-Natural Wind

Inspired by the lack of road illumination in our home, Puerto Rico, we identified a need that must be satisfied. The island calculates an expenditure of $120 dollars per light post, a cost of $112 million dollars annually as documented in the year 2012. The numbers demonstrate not only that this is an environmental problem, but an economic one as well, which we attempt to solve by bringing energy sustainability to our highways.

Our solution

We aimed for a sustainable and consistent energy source for our roads and we ended up using an unusual resource that for most people is invisible. We are talking about a non-natural renewable energy source, one that we produce with our daily activity, when we go to work, school, shopping, anywhere that involves you sitting in the car and driving to your destination. Every car in movement produces a pressure difference on the air, changing the way it moves, thus creating small air waves on its sights which are full of energy that until today has not been used.

Wind From Cars

The wind produced by a carâ€™s movement is a constant energy source, unlike solar panels or other power sources. Since its energy production is based on traffic patterns and not on natural wind, it does not depend on weather conditions and allows us to produce energy at any time. Using this energy is just an example of an unusual accomplishment on energy extraction which is exploiting a renewable non-natural resource. Utilizing renewable non-natural resources is something the humanity does not do often, something we will change with our systems.

Vertical Wind Turbines

Vertical wind turbines are the solution, for now, of transforming the wind energy from cars to electricity. Their ability of working at low and high wind speed makes them perfect for this specific job, since winds produced by one car can vary from 8 to 24mph without counting the wind speed reached by many cars at the same time, and the occasional combination with natural wind. Excluding wind speed as a variable, the vertical wind turbines are not affected by wind direction; their vertical axis allow them to use the wind no matter its direction, which is of great importance when using this source of energy.

Proof of Concept

Our system has many applications from highway illumination to power supply of near-street advertising billboards or other structures. To assure its functionality in different applications, we have constructed a functional prototype that has been tested at various street conditions such as highways, urban streets, coastal roads, and tunnels. We are currently testing a power storage system to guarantee constant energy while simultaneously working to make our first full system implementation.

Specific Dimensioning

After many filed tests and data collection of the winds produced by cars, we were able to make specific designs where the dimensions and the full aluminum structure of our vertical turbine is specifically made for the use of wind coming from transiting vehicles.
